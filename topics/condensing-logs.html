Condensing Logarithms

Key Properties:
Product Rule: \( \log_b(M) + \log_b(N) = \log_b(MN) \)
Quotient Rule: \( \log_b(M) - \log_b(N) = \log_b\!\left(\dfrac{M}{N}\right) \)
Power Rule: \( c \cdot \log_b(M) = \log_b(M^c) \)

Using the Product and Power Rules

1. Condense into a single logarithm: \( 3\log_2(x) + \log_2(5) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the power rule to the first term:

$$ \log_2(x^3) + \log_2(5) $$

Apply the product rule:

$$ \log_2(x^3 \cdot 5) $$

Answer: \( \log_2(5x^3) \)

2. Condense into a single logarithm: \( \log_3(7) + 2\log_3(x) + \log_3(y) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the power rule to the middle term:

$$ \log_3(7) + \log_3(x^2) + \log_3(y) $$

Apply the product rule to combine all three:

$$ \log_3(7 \cdot x^2 \cdot y) $$

Answer: \( \log_3(7x^2 y) \)

Using the Quotient Rule

3. Condense into a single logarithm: \( \log_4(x + 1) - \log_4(3x) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the quotient rule directly:

$$ \log_4\!\left(\frac{x + 1}{3x}\right) $$

Answer: \( \log_4\!\left(\dfrac{x + 1}{3x}\right) \)

4. Condense into a single logarithm: \( \log_5(2x - 6) - \log_5(x + 4) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the quotient rule:

$$ \log_5\!\left(\frac{2x - 6}{x + 4}\right) $$

Answer: \( \log_5\!\left(\dfrac{2x - 6}{x + 4}\right) \)

Combining All Three Rules

5. Condense into a single logarithm: \( 2\log(x) + 3\log(y) - \log(z) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the power rule to each term with a coefficient:

$$ \log(x^2) + \log(y^3) - \log(z) $$

Apply the product rule to the sum:

$$ \log(x^2 y^3) - \log(z) $$

Apply the quotient rule:

$$ \log\!\left(\frac{x^2 y^3}{z}\right) $$

Answer: \( \log\!\left(\dfrac{x^2 y^3}{z}\right) \)

6. Condense into a single logarithm: \( \tfrac{1}{2}\log_6(x) + \log_6(4) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the power rule to the first term:

$$ \log_6(x^{1/2}) + \log_6(4) = \log_6(\sqrt{x}) + \log_6(4) $$

Apply the product rule:

$$ \log_6(4\sqrt{x}) $$

Answer: \( \log_6(4\sqrt{x}) \)

7. Condense into a single logarithm: \( 3\ln(x) - 2\ln(y) + \ln(5) \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the power rule:

$$ \ln(x^3) - \ln(y^2) + \ln(5) $$

Combine the positive terms using the product rule:

$$ \ln(5x^3) - \ln(y^2) $$

Apply the quotient rule:

$$ \ln\!\left(\frac{5x^3}{y^2}\right) $$

Answer: \( \ln\!\left(\dfrac{5x^3}{y^2}\right) \)
