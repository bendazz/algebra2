Equation of a Line

Point-Slope Form: \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \)
Parallel lines have the same slope.
Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals: \( m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1 \).

Given a Point and a Slope

1. Find the equation of the line through \( (2, 5) \) with slope \( m = 3 \).
Reveal Solution

Use point-slope form with \( (x_1, y_1) = (2, 5) \) and \( m = 3 \):

$$ y - 5 = 3(x - 2) $$ $$ y - 5 = 3x - 6 $$ $$ y = 3x - 1 $$

Answer: \( y = 3x - 1 \)

2. Find the equation of the line through \( (-1, 4) \) with slope \( m = -\dfrac{1}{2} \).
Reveal Solution

Use point-slope form:

$$ y - 4 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - (-1)) $$ $$ y - 4 = -\frac{1}{2}(x + 1) $$ $$ y - 4 = -\frac{1}{2}x - \frac{1}{2} $$ $$ y = -\frac{1}{2}x + \frac{7}{2} $$

Answer: \( y = -\dfrac{1}{2}x + \dfrac{7}{2} \)

Parallel to a Given Line

3. Find the equation of the line through \( (6, -1) \) parallel to \( 2x - 5y = 10 \).
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Find the slope of the given line by rewriting in slope-intercept form:

$$ 2x - 5y = 10 $$ $$ -5y = -2x + 10 $$ $$ y = \frac{2}{5}x - 2 $$

The slope is \( m = \dfrac{2}{5} \). Parallel lines have the same slope.

Step 2 — Use point-slope form with \( (6, -1) \) and \( m = \dfrac{2}{5} \):

$$ y - (-1) = \frac{2}{5}(x - 6) $$ $$ y + 1 = \frac{2}{5}x - \frac{12}{5} $$ $$ y = \frac{2}{5}x - \frac{12}{5} - \frac{5}{5} = \frac{2}{5}x - \frac{17}{5} $$

Answer: \( y = \dfrac{2}{5}x - \dfrac{17}{5} \)

4. Find the equation of the line through \( (4, 3) \) parallel to \( y = -2x + 7 \).
Reveal Solution

The given line is already in slope-intercept form with slope \( m = -2 \). Parallel lines share the same slope.

Use point-slope form with \( (4, 3) \) and \( m = -2 \):

$$ y - 3 = -2(x - 4) $$ $$ y - 3 = -2x + 8 $$ $$ y = -2x + 11 $$

Answer: \( y = -2x + 11 \)

Perpendicular to a Given Line

5. Find the equation of the line through \( (3, -2) \) perpendicular to \( y = 3x + 1 \).
Reveal Solution

The given line has slope \( m = 3 \). The perpendicular slope is the negative reciprocal:

$$ m_\perp = -\frac{1}{3} $$

Use point-slope form with \( (3, -2) \):

$$ y - (-2) = -\frac{1}{3}(x - 3) $$ $$ y + 2 = -\frac{1}{3}x + 1 $$ $$ y = -\frac{1}{3}x - 1 $$

Answer: \( y = -\dfrac{1}{3}x - 1 \)

6. Find the equation of the line through \( (-2, 5) \) perpendicular to \( 4x + 3y = 12 \).
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Find the slope of the given line:

$$ 4x + 3y = 12 $$ $$ 3y = -4x + 12 $$ $$ y = -\frac{4}{3}x + 4 $$

The slope is \( m = -\dfrac{4}{3} \). The perpendicular slope is:

$$ m_\perp = \frac{3}{4} $$

Step 2 — Use point-slope form with \( (-2, 5) \):

$$ y - 5 = \frac{3}{4}(x - (-2)) $$ $$ y - 5 = \frac{3}{4}(x + 2) $$ $$ y - 5 = \frac{3}{4}x + \frac{3}{2} $$ $$ y = \frac{3}{4}x + \frac{13}{2} $$

Answer: \( y = \dfrac{3}{4}x + \dfrac{13}{2} \)
