Asymptotes

For each rational function, find all horizontal, vertical, and slant asymptotes. Write DNE if an asymptote type does not exist.

Degree of Numerator < Degree of Denominator

1. Find all asymptotes of \( f(x) = \dfrac{2x + 3}{x^2 - 4} \).
Reveal Solution

Vertical asymptotes: Set the denominator equal to zero:

$$ x^2 - 4 = 0 \implies (x-2)(x+2) = 0 $$

So \( x = 2 \) and \( x = -2 \).

Horizontal asymptote: The degree of the numerator (1) is less than the degree of the denominator (2), so:

$$ y = 0 $$

Slant asymptote: DNE (only occurs when the numerator's degree is exactly one more than the denominator's).

Vertical Asymptotes\( x = 2 \) and \( x = -2 \)
Horizontal Asymptote\( y = 0 \)
Slant AsymptoteDNE
2. Find all asymptotes of \( f(x) = \dfrac{3}{x^2 - x - 6} \).
Reveal Solution

Vertical asymptotes: Factor the denominator:

$$ x^2 - x - 6 = (x - 3)(x + 2) = 0 $$

So \( x = 3 \) and \( x = -2 \).

Horizontal asymptote: The degree of the numerator (0) is less than the degree of the denominator (2):

$$ y = 0 $$

Slant asymptote: DNE.

Vertical Asymptotes\( x = 3 \) and \( x = -2 \)
Horizontal Asymptote\( y = 0 \)
Slant AsymptoteDNE

Degree of Numerator = Degree of Denominator

3. Find all asymptotes of \( f(x) = \dfrac{4x^2 - 1}{x^2 + 3x} \).
Reveal Solution

Vertical asymptotes: Factor the denominator:

$$ x^2 + 3x = x(x + 3) = 0 $$

So \( x = 0 \) and \( x = -3 \).

Horizontal asymptote: The degrees are equal (both 2), so take the ratio of leading coefficients:

$$ y = \frac{4}{1} = 4 $$

Slant asymptote: DNE (degrees are equal, not one apart).

Vertical Asymptotes\( x = 0 \) and \( x = -3 \)
Horizontal Asymptote\( y = 4 \)
Slant AsymptoteDNE

Degree of Numerator = Degree of Denominator + 1 (Slant Asymptote)

5. Find all asymptotes of \( f(x) = \dfrac{2x^2 - x + 3}{x + 1} \).
Reveal Solution

Vertical asymptote: Set the denominator equal to zero:

$$ x + 1 = 0 \implies x = -1 $$

Horizontal asymptote: DNE (the degree of the numerator is greater than the degree of the denominator).

Slant asymptote: Since the numerator's degree (2) is exactly one more than the denominator's degree (1), perform polynomial long division:

Step 1. Divide the leading terms: \( 2x^2 \div x = 2x \).

Multiply: \( 2x(x + 1) = 2x^2 + 2x \). Subtract:

$$ (2x^2 - x + 3) - (2x^2 + 2x) = -3x + 3 $$

Step 2. Divide: \( -3x \div x = -3 \).

Multiply: \( -3(x + 1) = -3x - 3 \). Subtract:

$$ (-3x + 3) - (-3x - 3) = 6 $$

So:

$$ \frac{2x^2 - x + 3}{x + 1} = 2x - 3 + \frac{6}{x + 1} $$

As \( x \to \pm\infty \), the remainder \( \frac{6}{x+1} \to 0 \), so the slant asymptote is:

$$ y = 2x - 3 $$
Vertical Asymptote\( x = -1 \)
Horizontal AsymptoteDNE
Slant Asymptote\( y = 2x - 3 \)
6. Find all asymptotes of \( f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 + 2x - 1}{x - 3} \).
Reveal Solution

Vertical asymptote: Set the denominator equal to zero:

$$ x - 3 = 0 \implies x = 3 $$

Horizontal asymptote: DNE.

Slant asymptote: Perform polynomial long division:

Step 1. Divide the leading terms: \( x^2 \div x = x \).

Multiply: \( x(x - 3) = x^2 - 3x \). Subtract:

$$ (x^2 + 2x - 1) - (x^2 - 3x) = 5x - 1 $$

Step 2. Divide: \( 5x \div x = 5 \).

Multiply: \( 5(x - 3) = 5x - 15 \). Subtract:

$$ (5x - 1) - (5x - 15) = 14 $$

So:

$$ \frac{x^2 + 2x - 1}{x - 3} = x + 5 + \frac{14}{x - 3} $$

As \( x \to \pm\infty \), the remainder vanishes, so:

$$ y = x + 5 $$
Vertical Asymptote\( x = 3 \)
Horizontal AsymptoteDNE
Slant Asymptote\( y = x + 5 \)
