Vertex Form

Rewrite each parabola in vertex form \( y = a(x - h)^2 + k \) by completing the square. Identify the vertex and whether the parabola opens up or down.

Leading Coefficient a = 1

1. Rewrite in vertex form: \( y = x^2 + 6x + 5 \)
Reveal Solution

Group the \( x \)-terms and complete the square:

$$ y = (x^2 + 6x) + 5 $$

Take half of 6, which is 3, and square it: \( 3^2 = 9 \). Add and subtract 9:

$$ y = (x^2 + 6x + 9) - 9 + 5 $$ $$ y = (x + 3)^2 - 4 $$

The parabola opens up (\( a = 1 > 0 \)).

Vertex form: \( y = (x + 3)^2 - 4 \),   Vertex: \( (-3, -4) \)

2. Rewrite in vertex form: \( y = x^2 - 8x + 13 \)
Reveal Solution

Group the \( x \)-terms and complete the square:

$$ y = (x^2 - 8x) + 13 $$

Half of \(-8\) is \(-4\), and \((-4)^2 = 16\). Add and subtract 16:

$$ y = (x^2 - 8x + 16) - 16 + 13 $$ $$ y = (x - 4)^2 - 3 $$

The parabola opens up (\( a = 1 > 0 \)).

Vertex form: \( y = (x - 4)^2 - 3 \),   Vertex: \( (4, -3) \)

Leading Coefficient a > 1

3. Rewrite in vertex form: \( y = 2x^2 + 12x + 14 \)
Reveal Solution

Factor the leading coefficient out of the \( x \)-terms:

$$ y = 2(x^2 + 6x) + 14 $$

Complete the square inside the parentheses. Half of 6 is 3, and \( 3^2 = 9 \):

$$ y = 2(x^2 + 6x + 9) + 14 - 2(9) $$

Be careful — the 9 is multiplied by 2 when brought outside:

$$ y = 2(x + 3)^2 + 14 - 18 $$ $$ y = 2(x + 3)^2 - 4 $$

The parabola opens up (\( a = 2 > 0 \)) and is narrower than \( x^2 \).

Vertex form: \( y = 2(x + 3)^2 - 4 \),   Vertex: \( (-3, -4) \)

4. Rewrite in vertex form: \( y = 3x^2 - 18x + 23 \)
Reveal Solution

Factor the leading coefficient out of the \( x \)-terms:

$$ y = 3(x^2 - 6x) + 23 $$

Half of \(-6\) is \(-3\), and \((-3)^2 = 9\):

$$ y = 3(x^2 - 6x + 9) + 23 - 3(9) $$ $$ y = 3(x - 3)^2 + 23 - 27 $$ $$ y = 3(x - 3)^2 - 4 $$

The parabola opens up (\( a = 3 > 0 \)) and is narrower than \( x^2 \).

Vertex form: \( y = 3(x - 3)^2 - 4 \),   Vertex: \( (3, -4) \)

Negative Leading Coefficient

5. Rewrite in vertex form: \( y = -x^2 + 4x + 1 \)
Reveal Solution

Factor \(-1\) out of the \( x \)-terms:

$$ y = -(x^2 - 4x) + 1 $$

Half of \(-4\) is \(-2\), and \((-2)^2 = 4\):

$$ y = -(x^2 - 4x + 4) + 1 + 4 $$

The \(+4\) inside becomes \(+4\) outside because of the negative sign:

$$ y = -(x - 2)^2 + 5 $$

The parabola opens down (\( a = -1 < 0 \)), so the vertex is a maximum.

Vertex form: \( y = -(x - 2)^2 + 5 \),   Vertex: \( (2, 5) \)

6. Rewrite in vertex form: \( y = -2x^2 - 8x + 3 \)
Reveal Solution

Factor \(-2\) out of the \( x \)-terms:

$$ y = -2(x^2 + 4x) + 3 $$

Half of 4 is 2, and \( 2^2 = 4 \):

$$ y = -2(x^2 + 4x + 4) + 3 - (-2)(4) $$ $$ y = -2(x + 2)^2 + 3 + 8 $$ $$ y = -2(x + 2)^2 + 11 $$

The parabola opens down (\( a = -2 < 0 \)) and is narrower than \( x^2 \). The vertex is a maximum.

Vertex form: \( y = -2(x + 2)^2 + 11 \),   Vertex: \( (-2, 11) \)
