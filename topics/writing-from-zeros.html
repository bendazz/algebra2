Writing Functions from Zeros

Key Idea: If \( c \) is a zero of a polynomial, then \( (x - c) \) is a factor.
A zero with multiplicity \( k \) gives the factor \( (x - c)^k \).

Distinct Integer Zeros

1. Write a polynomial function with zeros \( 1, \, -3, \, 5 \).
Reveal Solution

Each zero gives a factor:

$$ f(x) = (x - 1)(x + 3)(x - 5) $$

Multiply the first two factors:

$$ (x - 1)(x + 3) = x^2 + 2x - 3 $$

Multiply by the third factor:

$$ (x^2 + 2x - 3)(x - 5) = x^3 - 5x^2 + 2x^2 - 10x - 3x + 15 $$ $$ = x^3 - 3x^2 - 13x + 15 $$

Answer: \( f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 - 13x + 15 \)

2. Write a polynomial function with zeros \( 0, \, -1, \, 4 \).
Reveal Solution

Each zero gives a factor (note: zero of \( 0 \) gives the factor \( x \)):

$$ f(x) = x(x + 1)(x - 4) $$

Multiply the last two factors:

$$ (x + 1)(x - 4) = x^2 - 3x - 4 $$

Multiply by \( x \):

$$ x(x^2 - 3x - 4) = x^3 - 3x^2 - 4x $$

Answer: \( f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 - 4x \)

Repeated Zeros (Multiplicity)

3. Write a polynomial function with zeros \( -2 \) (multiplicity 2) and \( 3 \).
Reveal Solution

The zero \( -2 \) has multiplicity 2, so its factor is squared:

$$ f(x) = (x + 2)^2(x - 3) $$

Expand \( (x + 2)^2 \):

$$ (x + 2)^2 = x^2 + 4x + 4 $$

Multiply by \( (x - 3) \):

$$ (x^2 + 4x + 4)(x - 3) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 4x^2 - 12x + 4x - 12 $$ $$ = x^3 + x^2 - 8x - 12 $$

Answer: \( f(x) = x^3 + x^2 - 8x - 12 \)

4. Write a polynomial function with zeros \( -4 \) and \( 1 \) (multiplicity 2).
Reveal Solution

The zero \( 1 \) has multiplicity 2:

$$ f(x) = (x + 4)(x - 1)^2 $$

Expand \( (x - 1)^2 \):

$$ (x - 1)^2 = x^2 - 2x + 1 $$

Multiply by \( (x + 4) \):

$$ (x + 4)(x^2 - 2x + 1) = x^3 - 2x^2 + x + 4x^2 - 8x + 4 $$ $$ = x^3 + 2x^2 - 7x + 4 $$

Answer: \( f(x) = x^3 + 2x^2 - 7x + 4 \)

More Challenging Zeros

5. Write a polynomial function with integer coefficients and zeros \( 0, \, \dfrac{1}{2}, \, -3 \).
Reveal Solution

Start with the factors from each zero:

$$ f(x) = x\!\left(x - \tfrac{1}{2}\right)(x + 3) $$

To get integer coefficients, rewrite \( \left(x - \frac{1}{2}\right) \) as \( \frac{1}{2}(2x - 1) \). Since we only need a polynomial with these zeros, we can use \( (2x - 1) \) instead:

$$ f(x) = x(2x - 1)(x + 3) $$

Multiply the last two factors:

$$ (2x - 1)(x + 3) = 2x^2 + 6x - x - 3 = 2x^2 + 5x - 3 $$

Multiply by \( x \):

$$ x(2x^2 + 5x - 3) = 2x^3 + 5x^2 - 3x $$

Answer: \( f(x) = 2x^3 + 5x^2 - 3x \)

6. Write a polynomial function with zeros \( -2, \, -1, \, 1, \, 2 \).
Reveal Solution

Write the four factors:

$$ f(x) = (x + 2)(x + 1)(x - 1)(x - 2) $$

Notice we can pair them using the difference of squares:

$$ (x + 2)(x - 2) = x^2 - 4 $$ $$ (x + 1)(x - 1) = x^2 - 1 $$

Multiply the two results:

$$ (x^2 - 4)(x^2 - 1) = x^4 - x^2 - 4x^2 + 4 $$ $$ = x^4 - 5x^2 + 4 $$

Answer: \( f(x) = x^4 - 5x^2 + 4 \)
