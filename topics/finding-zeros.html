Finding Zeros

Find all zeros of each polynomial function.

Factor by Grouping

1. Find all zeros of \( f(x) = x^3 + 5x^2 - 9x - 45 \).
Reveal Solution

Group the terms in pairs:

$$ x^2(x + 5) - 9(x + 5) = 0 $$

Factor out the common binomial:

$$ (x^2 - 9)(x + 5) = 0 $$

Recognize the difference of squares:

$$ (x - 3)(x + 3)(x + 5) = 0 $$

Zeros: \( x = 3, \; x = -3, \; x = -5 \)

2. Find all zeros of \( f(x) = x^3 - 2x^2 - 25x + 50 \).
Reveal Solution

Group the terms in pairs:

$$ x^2(x - 2) - 25(x - 2) = 0 $$

Factor out the common binomial:

$$ (x^2 - 25)(x - 2) = 0 $$

Factor the difference of squares:

$$ (x - 5)(x + 5)(x - 2) = 0 $$

Zeros: \( x = 5, \; x = -5, \; x = 2 \)

Rational Root Theorem & Synthetic Division

3. Find all zeros of \( f(x) = 2x^3 - 3x^2 - 11x + 6 \).
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — List possible rational zeros using \( \dfrac{p}{q} \), where \( p \) divides the constant term 6 and \( q \) divides the leading coefficient 2:

$$ \pm 1, \; \pm 2, \; \pm 3, \; \pm 6, \; \pm \tfrac{1}{2}, \; \pm \tfrac{3}{2} $$

Step 2 — Test candidates. Try \( x = 3 \):

$$ f(3) = 2(27) - 3(9) - 11(3) + 6 = 54 - 27 - 33 + 6 = 0 \; \checkmark $$

Step 3 — Synthetic division by \( (x - 3) \):

$$ \begin{array}{c|cccc} 3 & 2 & -3 & -11 & 6 \\ & & 6 & 9 & -6 \\ \hline & 2 & 3 & -2 & 0 \end{array} $$

The quotient is \( 2x^2 + 3x - 2 \). Factor it:

$$ 2x^2 + 3x - 2 = (2x - 1)(x + 2) $$

So the complete factorization is:

$$ f(x) = (x - 3)(2x - 1)(x + 2) $$

Zeros: \( x = 3, \; x = \dfrac{1}{2}, \; x = -2 \)

4. Find all zeros of \( f(x) = x^4 - 5x^2 + 4 \).
Reveal Solution

This is a quadratic in disguise. Let \( u = x^2 \):

$$ u^2 - 5u + 4 = 0 $$ $$ (u - 1)(u - 4) = 0 $$

So \( u = 1 \) or \( u = 4 \). Substitute back:

$$ x^2 = 1 \implies x = \pm 1 $$ $$ x^2 = 4 \implies x = \pm 2 $$

Complete factorization:

$$ f(x) = (x - 1)(x + 1)(x - 2)(x + 2) $$

Zeros: \( x = -2, \; x = -1, \; x = 1, \; x = 2 \)

Complex Zeros

When synthetic division reduces a polynomial to a quadratic with a negative discriminant (\( b^2 - 4ac < 0 \)), the remaining zeros are complex. Remember: \( \sqrt{-1} = i \), and complex zeros always come in conjugate pairs \( a + bi \) and \( a - bi \).

5. Find all zeros (real and complex) of \( f(x) = x^4 + x^3 - x^2 + x - 2 \).
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Possible rational zeros: \( \pm 1, \; \pm 2 \).

Step 2 — Test \( x = 1 \):

$$ f(1) = 1 + 1 - 1 + 1 - 2 = 0 \; \checkmark $$

Step 3 — Synthetic division by \( (x - 1) \):

$$ \begin{array}{c|ccccc} 1 & 1 & 1 & -1 & 1 & -2 \\ & & 1 & 2 & 1 & 2 \\ \hline & 1 & 2 & 1 & 2 & 0 \end{array} $$

Quotient: \( x^3 + 2x^2 + x + 2 \). Factor by grouping:

$$ x^2(x + 2) + 1(x + 2) = (x^2 + 1)(x + 2) $$

Step 4 — Solve \( x^2 + 1 = 0 \):

$$ x^2 = -1 \implies x = \pm\, i $$

Complete factorization:

$$ f(x) = (x - 1)(x + 2)(x^2 + 1) $$

Zeros: \( x = 1, \; x = -2, \; x = i, \; x = -i \)

6. Find all zeros (real and complex) of \( f(x) = x^4 - 2x^3 + 10x^2 - 18x + 9 \).
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Possible rational zeros: \( \pm 1, \; \pm 3, \; \pm 9 \).

Step 2 — Test \( x = 1 \):

$$ f(1) = 1 - 2 + 10 - 18 + 9 = 0 \; \checkmark $$

Step 3 — Synthetic division by \( (x - 1) \):

$$ \begin{array}{c|ccccc} 1 & 1 & -2 & 10 & -18 & 9 \\ & & 1 & -1 & 9 & -9 \\ \hline & 1 & -1 & 9 & -9 & 0 \end{array} $$

Quotient: \( x^3 - x^2 + 9x - 9 \). Test \( x = 1 \) again:

$$ 1 - 1 + 9 - 9 = 0 \; \checkmark $$

Step 4 — Divide again by \( (x - 1) \):

$$ \begin{array}{c|cccc} 1 & 1 & -1 & 9 & -9 \\ & & 1 & 0 & 9 \\ \hline & 1 & 0 & 9 & 0 \end{array} $$

Quotient: \( x^2 + 9 \). Solve \( x^2 + 9 = 0 \):

$$ x^2 = -9 \implies x = \pm\, 3i $$

Complete factorization:

$$ f(x) = (x - 1)^2(x^2 + 9) $$

Zeros: \( x = 1 \) (multiplicity 2), \( x = 3i, \; x = -3i \)

7. Find all zeros (real and complex) of \( f(x) = x^4 - 6x^3 + 14x^2 - 14x + 5 \).
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Possible rational zeros: \( \pm 1, \; \pm 5 \).

Step 2 — Test \( x = 1 \):

$$ f(1) = 1 - 6 + 14 - 14 + 5 = 0 \; \checkmark $$

Step 3 — Synthetic division by \( (x - 1) \):

$$ \begin{array}{c|ccccc} 1 & 1 & -6 & 14 & -14 & 5 \\ & & 1 & -5 & 9 & -5 \\ \hline & 1 & -5 & 9 & -5 & 0 \end{array} $$

Quotient: \( x^3 - 5x^2 + 9x - 5 \). Test \( x = 1 \) again:

$$ 1 - 5 + 9 - 5 = 0 \; \checkmark $$

Step 4 — Divide again by \( (x - 1) \):

$$ \begin{array}{c|cccc} 1 & 1 & -5 & 9 & -5 \\ & & 1 & -4 & 5 \\ \hline & 1 & -4 & 5 & 0 \end{array} $$

Quotient: \( x^2 - 4x + 5 \). Use the quadratic formula:

$$ x = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16 - 20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{-4}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i $$

Complete factorization:

$$ f(x) = (x - 1)^2(x^2 - 4x + 5) $$

Zeros: \( x = 1 \) (multiplicity 2), \( x = 2 + i, \; x = 2 - i \)
