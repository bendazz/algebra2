Domains of Functions

Key Restrictions:
Even roots (square root, 4th root, …): the radicand must be \( \geq 0 \).
Odd roots (cube root, 5th root, …): defined for all real numbers.
Rational functions: the denominator must be \( \neq 0 \).

Square Root Functions

1. Find the domain of \( f(x) = \sqrt{3x + 12} \).
Reveal Solution

The expression under the square root must be non-negative:

$$ 3x + 12 \geq 0 $$ $$ 3x \geq -12 $$ $$ x \geq -4 $$

Domain: \( [-4, \infty) \)

2. Find the domain of \( f(x) = \sqrt{7 - 2x} \).
Reveal Solution

Set the radicand \( \geq 0 \):

$$ 7 - 2x \geq 0 $$ $$ -2x \geq -7 $$

Divide by \( -2 \) and flip the inequality:

$$ x \leq \frac{7}{2} $$

Domain: \( \left(-\infty,\, \dfrac{7}{2}\right] \)

Cube Root Functions

3. Find the domain of \( g(x) = \sqrt[3]{2x + 5} \).
Reveal Solution

A cube root is an odd root, so it is defined for all real numbers. There is no restriction on \( 2x + 5 \) — it can be positive, negative, or zero.

Domain: \( (-\infty, \infty) \)

Rational Functions

4. Find the domain of \( h(x) = \dfrac{3}{x^2 + 4x - 21} \).
Reveal Solution

The denominator cannot equal zero. Factor and solve:

$$ x^2 + 4x - 21 = (x + 7)(x - 3) = 0 $$ $$ x = -7 \quad \text{or} \quad x = 3 $$

Exclude these values from the domain.

Domain: \( (-\infty, -7) \cup (-7, 3) \cup (3, \infty) \)

5. Find the domain of \( h(x) = \dfrac{x + 1}{x^2 - 9} \).
Reveal Solution

Set the denominator \( \neq 0 \):

$$ x^2 - 9 = (x - 3)(x + 3) = 0 $$ $$ x = 3 \quad \text{or} \quad x = -3 $$

Exclude these values.

Domain: \( (-\infty, -3) \cup (-3, 3) \cup (3, \infty) \)

Combined Restrictions

6. Find the domain of \( f(x) = \dfrac{\sqrt{x + 2}}{x - 5} \).
Reveal Solution

There are two restrictions to satisfy simultaneously:

1. Square root: The radicand must be non-negative:

$$ x + 2 \geq 0 \implies x \geq -2 $$

2. Denominator: Cannot equal zero:

$$ x - 5 \neq 0 \implies x \neq 5 $$

Combine both: \( x \geq -2 \) and \( x \neq 5 \).

Domain: \( [-2, 5) \cup (5, \infty) \)
