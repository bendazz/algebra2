Solving Equations

Quadratic Equations

1. Solve: \( 3x^2 - 10x + 6 = 0 \)
Reveal Solution

Use the quadratic formula with \( a=3,\; b=-10,\; c=6 \):

$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} = \frac{10 \pm \sqrt{100 - 72}}{6} = \frac{10 \pm \sqrt{28}}{6} $$

Simplify \( \sqrt{28} = 2\sqrt{7} \):

$$ x = \frac{10 \pm 2\sqrt{7}}{6} = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{7}}{3} $$

Answer: \( x = \dfrac{5 + \sqrt{7}}{3} \) or \( x = \dfrac{5 - \sqrt{7}}{3} \)

2. Solve: \( 5x^2 + 2x - 4 = 0 \)
Reveal Solution

Apply the quadratic formula with \( a=5,\; b=2,\; c=-4 \):

$$ x = \frac{-2 \pm \sqrt{4 + 80}}{10} = \frac{-2 \pm \sqrt{84}}{10} $$

Simplify \( \sqrt{84} = 2\sqrt{21} \):

$$ x = \frac{-2 \pm 2\sqrt{21}}{10} = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{21}}{5} $$

Answer: \( x = \dfrac{-1 + \sqrt{21}}{5} \) or \( x = \dfrac{-1 - \sqrt{21}}{5} \)

Factor by Grouping

3. Solve: \( 8x^3 + 12x^2 - 2x - 3 = 0 \)
Reveal Solution

Group the terms in pairs:

$$ (8x^3 + 12x^2) + (-2x - 3) = 0 $$

Factor each group:

$$ 4x^2(2x + 3) - 1(2x + 3) = 0 $$

Factor out the common binomial:

$$ (4x^2 - 1)(2x + 3) = 0 $$

Recognize \( 4x^2 - 1 \) as a difference of squares:

$$ (2x - 1)(2x + 1)(2x + 3) = 0 $$

Answer: \( x = \dfrac{1}{2},\; x = -\dfrac{1}{2},\; x = -\dfrac{3}{2} \)

4. Solve: \( 27x^3 + 18x^2 - 3x - 2 = 0 \)
Reveal Solution

Group the terms in pairs:

$$ (27x^3 + 18x^2) + (-3x - 2) = 0 $$

Factor each group:

$$ 9x^2(3x + 2) - 1(3x + 2) = 0 $$

Factor out the common binomial:

$$ (9x^2 - 1)(3x + 2) = 0 $$

Recognize \( 9x^2 - 1 \) as a difference of squares:

$$ (3x - 1)(3x + 1)(3x + 2) = 0 $$

Answer: \( x = \dfrac{1}{3},\; x = -\dfrac{1}{3},\; x = -\dfrac{2}{3} \)

Exponential Equations

5. Solve: \( 3^{2x+1} = 243 \)
Reveal Solution

Rewrite 243 as a power of 3:

$$ 3^{2x+1} = 3^5 $$

Since the bases are equal, set the exponents equal:

$$ 2x + 1 = 5 $$ $$ 2x = 4 $$

Answer: \( x = 2 \)

6. Solve: \( 5^{4x-3} = 625 \)
Reveal Solution

Rewrite 625 as a power of 5:

$$ 5^{4x-3} = 5^4 $$

Set the exponents equal:

$$ 4x - 3 = 4 $$ $$ 4x = 7 $$

Answer: \( x = \dfrac{7}{4} \)

Exponential Equations (Fractional Bases)

7. Solve: \( \left(\dfrac{1}{9}\right)^x = 3^{x+4} \)
Reveal Solution

Rewrite \( \frac{1}{9} \) as \( 3^{-2} \):

$$ \left(3^{-2}\right)^x = 3^{x+4} $$ $$ 3^{-2x} = 3^{x+4} $$

Set the exponents equal:

$$ -2x = x + 4 $$ $$ -3x = 4 $$

Answer: \( x = -\dfrac{4}{3} \)

8. Solve: \( \left(\dfrac{1}{8}\right)^x = 2^{2x+9} \)
Reveal Solution

Rewrite \( \frac{1}{8} \) as \( 2^{-3} \):

$$ \left(2^{-3}\right)^x = 2^{2x+9} $$ $$ 2^{-3x} = 2^{2x+9} $$

Set the exponents equal:

$$ -3x = 2x + 9 $$ $$ -5x = 9 $$

Answer: \( x = -\dfrac{9}{5} \)

Logarithmic Equations

9. Solve: \( \log(5x) + \log(x - 1) = 2 \)
Reveal Solution

Use the product rule for logarithms:

$$ \log\bigl(5x(x - 1)\bigr) = 2 $$

Convert to exponential form (\(\log\) means base 10):

$$ 5x(x - 1) = 10^2 = 100 $$ $$ 5x^2 - 5x = 100 $$ $$ x^2 - x - 20 = 0 $$ $$ (x - 5)(x + 4) = 0 $$

So \( x = 5 \) or \( x = -4 \). Check the domain: we need \( 5x > 0 \) and \( x - 1 > 0 \), so \( x > 1 \). Reject \( x = -4 \).

Answer: \( x = 5 \)

10. Solve: \( \log(3x) + \log(x + 1) = \log(18) \)
Reveal Solution

Use the product rule on the left side:

$$ \log\bigl(3x(x + 1)\bigr) = \log(18) $$

Since the logs are equal, the arguments are equal:

$$ 3x(x + 1) = 18 $$ $$ 3x^2 + 3x - 18 = 0 $$ $$ x^2 + x - 6 = 0 $$ $$ (x + 3)(x - 2) = 0 $$

So \( x = 2 \) or \( x = -3 \). Check the domain: we need \( 3x > 0 \) and \( x + 1 > 0 \), so \( x > 0 \). Reject \( x = -3 \).

Answer: \( x = 2 \)

Logarithmic Equations (Other Bases)

11. Solve: \( 5 + 3\log_2(x - 1) = 17 \)
Reveal Solution

Isolate the logarithm:

$$ 3\log_2(x - 1) = 12 $$ $$ \log_2(x - 1) = 4 $$

Convert to exponential form:

$$ x - 1 = 2^4 = 16 $$

Answer: \( x = 17 \)

12. Solve: \( 1 + 4\log_5(2x + 3) = 9 \)
Reveal Solution

Isolate the logarithm:

$$ 4\log_5(2x + 3) = 8 $$ $$ \log_5(2x + 3) = 2 $$

Convert to exponential form:

$$ 2x + 3 = 5^2 = 25 $$ $$ 2x = 22 $$

Answer: \( x = 11 \)

Absolute Value Equations

13. Solve: \( |3x + 4| = 7 \)
Reveal Solution

Set up two cases:

Case 1: \( 3x + 4 = 7 \)

$$ 3x = 3 \implies x = 1 $$

Case 2: \( 3x + 4 = -7 \)

$$ 3x = -11 \implies x = -\frac{11}{3} $$

Answer: \( x = 1 \) or \( x = -\dfrac{11}{3} \)

14. Solve: \( |5x - 2| = 12 \)
Reveal Solution

Set up two cases:

Case 1: \( 5x - 2 = 12 \)

$$ 5x = 14 \implies x = \frac{14}{5} $$

Case 2: \( 5x - 2 = -12 \)

$$ 5x = -10 \implies x = -2 $$

Answer: \( x = \dfrac{14}{5} \) or \( x = -2 \)

Radical Equations

15. Solve: \( \sqrt{2x + 5} = x + 1 \)
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Square both sides to eliminate the radical:

$$ 2x + 5 = (x + 1)^2 $$ $$ 2x + 5 = x^2 + 2x + 1 $$

Step 2 — Rearrange into standard form:

$$ 0 = x^2 + 2x + 1 - 2x - 5 $$ $$ 0 = x^2 - 4 $$ $$ (x - 2)(x + 2) = 0 $$ $$ x = 2 \quad \text{or} \quad x = -2 $$

Step 3 — Check for extraneous solutions (squaring can introduce false solutions!):

\( x = 2 \): \( \sqrt{2(2)+5} = \sqrt{9} = 3 \) and \( 2+1 = 3 \;\checkmark \)

\( x = -2 \): \( \sqrt{2(-2)+5} = \sqrt{1} = 1 \) and \( -2+1 = -1 \;\boldsymbol{\times} \)

Since \( 1 \neq -1 \), the solution \( x = -2 \) is extraneous.

Answer: \( x = 2 \)

16. Solve: \( \sqrt{7 - x} = x + 5 \)
Reveal Solution

Step 1 — Square both sides:

$$ 7 - x = (x + 5)^2 $$ $$ 7 - x = x^2 + 10x + 25 $$

Step 2 — Rearrange:

$$ 0 = x^2 + 10x + 25 + x - 7 $$ $$ 0 = x^2 + 11x + 18 $$ $$ (x + 2)(x + 9) = 0 $$ $$ x = -2 \quad \text{or} \quad x = -9 $$

Step 3 — Check for extraneous solutions:

\( x = -2 \): \( \sqrt{7-(-2)} = \sqrt{9} = 3 \) and \( -2+5 = 3 \;\checkmark \)

\( x = -9 \): \( \sqrt{7-(-9)} = \sqrt{16} = 4 \) and \( -9+5 = -4 \;\boldsymbol{\times} \)

Since \( 4 \neq -4 \), the solution \( x = -9 \) is extraneous.

Answer: \( x = -2 \)
